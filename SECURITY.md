# Security Policy

## Reporting a Vulnerability

If you believe you have found a security vulnerability in Saturn, please report it to us privately. **Do not open a public GitHub issue for security bugs.**

Send your report to: **security@saturn-pay.com**

Include as much of the following as possible:

- A description of the vulnerability and its potential impact.
- Steps to reproduce the issue or a proof-of-concept.
- Any relevant logs, screenshots, or configuration details.

## Scope

The following are in scope for security reports:

- The Saturn API server.
- The Saturn SDK.
- Payment infrastructure and transaction processing.
- Authentication and authorization mechanisms.
- Data handling and storage of sensitive payment information.

## Out of Scope

The following are **not** in scope:

- Social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing) against Saturn employees or users.
- Denial-of-service (DoS) attacks against Saturn infrastructure.
- Issues in third-party dependencies that are not directly exploitable through Saturn.
- Findings from automated scanners without a demonstrated proof of impact.

## Response Timeline

- **Acknowledgment**: We will acknowledge receipt of your report within **48 hours**.
- **Triage**: We will assess the severity and validity of the report within **7 days**.
- **Resolution**: We will work to resolve confirmed vulnerabilities as quickly as possible and will keep you informed of our progress.

## Responsible Disclosure

We ask that you:

- Allow us reasonable time to investigate and address the issue before making any public disclosure.
- Avoid accessing or modifying data that does not belong to you.
- Act in good faith to avoid disruption to Saturn services and our users.

## Credit

We value the work of security researchers. Reporters who follow this responsible disclosure policy will be credited for their findings, unless they prefer to remain anonymous.

## Contact

For all security-related inquiries: **security@saturn-pay.com**
